About

Stian Larsen is a researcher and practitioner dedicated to understanding how strength training works — from the biomechanics of a barbell back squat to the mechanisms of muscle hypertrophy.

He holds a PhD in professional science from Nord University, where he specialized in musculoskeletal modeling and biomechanics during the barbell back squat. With over 41 peer-reviewed publications and 400+ citations, his work bridges the gap between laboratory science and real-world training.

As Assisting Head of Education at AFPT (Akademiet for Personlig Trening), Stian plays a key role in shaping Norway's evidence-based curriculum for personal trainers. He joined AFPT as a PT student in 2019 and has been a lecturer since 2021.

Beyond academia, Stian is an active strength coach with hands-on experience working with athletes focused on muscle hypertrophy and maximal strength development.

Research

Stian's research sits at the intersection of biomechanics and applied strength training — with a particular focus on how exercise selection, technique, and programming influence muscle hypertrophy and strength performance.

🏋️

Squat Biomechanics

Investigating how barbell placement, stance width, and load affect kinematics, kinetics, and muscle activation in the back squat — including the sticking region.

💪

Muscle Hypertrophy

Exploring how exercise selection, range of motion, and training variables influence skeletal muscle growth — from the quadriceps to the deltoids and calves.

📊

Training Methodology

Systematic reviews and meta-analyses on autoregulation, free weights vs. machines, drop sets, and training beyond momentary failure.

🔬

Exercise Science

Broad contributions to exercise science including EMG methodology, anthropometric predictors of strength, and fitness centre participation motives.
