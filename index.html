About

Bridging the gap between the lab and the lifting platform.

Dr. Stian Larsen is a Norwegian sports scientist whose work sits at the intersection of rigorous academic research and practical strength training. Holding a PhD in Professional Science from Nord University — with a specialization in squat biomechanics — he brings a depth of understanding to movement mechanics that is rare in both the academic and coaching worlds.

As Assistant Academic Director and Lecturer at AFPT (Akademiet For Personlig Trening), Stian shapes the next generation of evidence-based personal trainers in Norway. His teaching philosophy is straightforward: great coaching must be grounded in science, and great science must speak to the real world.

With over 30 peer-reviewed publications to his name and certified expertise spanning personal training, rehabilitation, and nutrition, Stian is one of Scandinavia's leading voices in applied strength and conditioning science.

Expertise

Core areas of mastery

Three pillars that define Stian's scientific and educational work.

🦴

Biomechanics

Specializing in squat biomechanics, Stian investigates how joint mechanics, bar path, stance width, and individual anatomy interact to produce efficient and safe movement under load. His PhD research pushed the frontier of what we know about human movement in strength sports.

Squat Mechanics Joint Kinematics Force Analysis Movement Science
💪

Muscle Hypertrophy

A deep researcher in the mechanisms of skeletal muscle growth, Stian explores how training variables — volume, load, tempo, and frequency — interact with physiological processes to maximize muscle adaptation. His work bridges bench research and gym-floor application.

Muscle Growth Training Variables Muscle Physiology Resistance Training
📊

Evidence-Based Training

Stian champions the translation of research into practice — critically evaluating the literature to develop programming frameworks that actually work. His educational role at AFPT ensures this evidence-based approach reaches practitioners across Norway.

Strength Programming Research Translation Coaching Science Pedagogy
